About This Service

You cannot fix what you cannot measure. Indoor air quality testing gives you hard data on what is circulating through your home, from particulate matter and mold spores to volatile organic compounds and carbon dioxide levels. In Los Angeles, where outdoor air quality regularly ranks among the worst in the nation, knowing your indoor levels is especially important.

Our technicians use calibrated professional-grade monitors to sample the air in multiple rooms of your home. We measure PM2.5 and PM10 particulate levels, relative humidity, CO2 concentration, and VOC levels. Results are compiled into a clear, easy-to-read report with specific recommendations for improving your air quality.

Air quality testing is an ideal first step before scheduling duct cleaning, HVAC service, or adding filtration upgrades. It is also valuable after renovations, water damage events, or if household members are experiencing unexplained allergy symptoms. Win Air Cleaning provides honest assessments and only recommends services that will actually make a difference.

Frequently Asked Questions

What pollutants do you test for?

We measure fine and coarse particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10), carbon dioxide, volatile organic compounds, relative humidity, and temperature. If you suspect mold, we can add mold spore sampling to the assessment.

How long does air quality testing take?

A typical residential test takes about 1 to 2 hours. We sample the air in your main living areas, bedrooms, and near your HVAC system to get a complete picture of your indoor environment.

Will testing tell me if I need duct cleaning?

It is one of the best ways to find out. High particulate levels often point to dirty ductwork or a failing filter. We use your results to make honest recommendations, and we never push services your home does not need.
